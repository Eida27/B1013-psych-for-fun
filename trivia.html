Psychology Trivia

Quick facts for curious minds.

Bite-sized behavior notes that can become story ideas, conversation starters, or reminders to pause before judging.

01

Uncertainty can feel heavier than bad news.

The mind keeps checking unfinished situations, which is why vague silence can feel more stressful than a clear answer.

02

People remember emotional endings.

A final moment can color how someone recalls an entire experience, even when most of the event was ordinary.

03

Groups often borrow confidence.

When facts are unclear, a steady voice can become a shortcut for trust, even before evidence catches up.

04

Small rewards can train big habits.

Tiny moments of relief, attention, or approval can quietly teach a person to repeat the same behavior.

05

Memory is reconstruction, not replay.

People often rebuild memories from fragments, feelings, and later information instead of replaying a perfect recording.

06

Rejection can make neutral cues look personal.

After a social sting, the brain may scan harder for signs of danger and mistake ordinary distance for dislike.
